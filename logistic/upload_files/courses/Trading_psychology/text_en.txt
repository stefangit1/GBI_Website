    <h2 id="einleitung">1 Introduction</h2>
    <p>Why is this topic so important that it deserves its own section? Many mistakes and losses occur not because of the strategies, but due to undisciplined actions. There are several ways to overcome these poor decisions. Our two main culprits here are emotion and impatience. Another antagonist in our story is laziness. But now we will bring you closer to the most common mistakes to save you from major misfortunes.</p>

    <h2 id="fehler">2 Mistake: "It was this way, and it will always be this way"</h2>
    <p>Once upon a time, a fisherman lived in a boat hut on a large lake, which we call Moneysee. He fished every day and caught well every day, so he never thought about what would happen if he suddenly stopped catching fish. Until one day, out of the blue, the temperature drastically increased, causing the plankton in the lake to multiply radically, and all the fish died. He still had enough fish for today, but the lake was dead and he had no food source left. The rest of the story is for you to interpret.</p>
    <p>The moral is that just because things are going well now, it doesn’t mean they will always be this way. You need a backup or a plan B. Relying on the current situation is too risky, as everything can change at any moment.</p>

    <h2 id="nachrennen">3 Problem of Chasing Losses</h2>
    <p>Once a loss is recorded, people frantically chase the lost capital, hoping to return to their previous balance. Two figures emerge from the shadows in this action: Mr. Emotion and Mrs. Impatience. Mr. Emotion is angry and frustrated. He feels bad because he doesn’t like losing money. He tries with all his might, in a certain self-desperation, to recover the loss. Mrs. Impatience, Mr. Emotion’s wife, agrees with her husband but needs the money urgently, otherwise she can’t sleep at night. What the two forget is that this method almost never works and can even make the situation worse. Aside from the undisciplined approach, they forget that they need to make 100% profit to cover the 50% loss. This means that if you lose half, you need to double your capital to break even. However, this is easier said than done, and with these two figures in mind, you may be tempted to make even bigger mistakes. Therefore, it is important to stop after a loss and relax a bit to clear your mind. Alternatively, you can postpone trading to the next day, which might even be more efficient.</p>

    <h2 id="wissen">4 Believing You Know is Not Knowing</h2>
    <p>Often, we see traders in various social media courses who believe they can predict what will happen. They see some patterns and try to market them better through shown profits or trades they “must share”. While we can read from the chart what might happen and increase the probability of a correct outcome, it will never be 100%. Do not misinterpret this by thinking we claim technical analysis doesn’t work. No, we claim that you should avoid the thought that it is certain and 100% correct in which direction the trend is going. Always keep in mind that believing you know and the calculation might not work out, because if you risk too much based on the belief that you can see into the future, it can quickly end and lead to significant losses. However, with good risk management, which we will cover in another course, this problem should also be manageable.</p>

    <h2 id="schnellesGeld">5 Quick Money</h2>
    <p>Many start trading because they think they can quickly and easily become very rich. However, the path to actual consistent success in trading is a very long one and requires a lot of learning and practice. Some might achieve real success within a few months, but on average, it can take 1 to 3 years to earn money professionally. Do not be discouraged by people who claim, “I made thousands of euros in one month with trading”. Either you are being lied to, or someone is trying to sell you a course. There are also several good proverbs for this: “Good things take time” or “Only weeds grow quickly”. Stick to these sayings and you will find that consistent improvement over a longer period makes more sense than quick money.</p>

    <h2 id="alleWege">6 All Roads Lead to Rome</h2>
    <p>A nice proverb that takes on even more meaning in trading. “All roads lead to Rome, some take longer and some shorter, some are rocky and some are smooth. But anyone who doesn’t deviate from their path to Rome will eventually arrive.” What do we mean by this? Everyone has their own methodology for trying to make money in trading. There is no right or wrong here, as one path might be shorter but uphill, while another might be longer but a straight road. Depending on which path you choose, you need to follow it. If you deviate from the path or want to choose a different one in the meantime, you may get lost or have to start over from scratch. Lost time and effort. So stick to how you want to trade. It is logical to adjust your strategy and optimize values, but do not switch to a completely new direction just because the current path seems too long or difficult.</p>

    <h2 id="system">7 The System Behind It</h2>
    <p>Many start directly with technical analysis and do not really understand what is happening. They do not understand the coherence between currencies and commodities (USD/OIL), which news has what impact on the economy, how to handle risk (risk management), how the price forms, and many other elements that one should actually learn. Most beginners are only interested in how to draw lines on the chart that some YouTuber, who needs to sell courses because they earn too little from trading, explained. Dear readers, we ask you to try to understand the system behind it. Buy books about trading or general finance to understand economic policy. Books contain the world’s knowledge because only a fraction of that information can be found on the internet. Since we do not want to advertise for authors, if you are interested in quality books, please send us an email or call our hotline, and we will give you some good tips.</p>

    <h2 id="muster">8 The Problem with Patterns</h2>
    <p>Almost everyone who has been involved in stock/forex/commodity trading knows them: the specific patterns. They keep appearing on the chart, looking like an M, W, a channel, a flag, or other symbols. But what is the problem with this? After all, patterns have always existed and they work, right? They may work, but we need to speculate a bit. When we scroll back through the chart in the past, we will always recognize patterns and draw them, identifying the subsequent rules. But when we go back to the present, where the price is currently forming, what do we see? Nothing, right? We can only speculate that it will be this or that pattern, but we can’t know for sure. Once a pattern is formed, we exclaim, “I knew it,” but if it doesn’t, we forget that we were trying to see something that wasn’t there. Another problem is that while the pattern is forming, we do not know which one is currently forming, and once we see what it really is, it might be too late. We do not want to discourage you from trading with patterns; we just want to question this topic. If it works for you and you want to pursue this path, that is completely legitimate, as mentioned in the previous topic, “All Roads Lead to Rome.”</p>

    <h2 id="stagnation">9 Stagnant Profits and Losses</h2>
    <p>You have reached a point where you don’t know what you’re doing wrong. You are not losing money, but you are also not making any. Sometimes there are profits and sometimes losses, to the same extent. What now? This is a difficult question because many people despair at this point and give up. You need to see it positively because you are at the tipping point to becoming a profitable trader. But from here, no one can help you further. It is possible to keep learning and improving, but from now on, you rely on your own strategy. The only advice we can give you is to document everything you do. Document your daily condition, the trades, the approach, and be disciplined. Once you break through the barrier, it will only go up from there. I wish you much success.</p>
